Understanding Phishing

Learn the basics about phishing attacks and how to protect yourself.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where attackers impersonate legitimate individuals or organizations through email, text messages, or other communication channels to steal sensitive information. This can include login credentials, credit card numbers, bank account details, or personally identifiable information (PII).

Common Types of Phishing

  • Email Phishing: The most common form, using emails that appear to be from trusted sources.
  • Spear Phishing: A targeted attack aimed at a specific individual or organization.
  • Whaling: A type of spear phishing specifically targeting high-profile executives.
  • Smishing: Phishing conducted via SMS text messages.
  • Vishing: Phishing conducted over voice calls (Voice Phishing).

How to Identify a Phishing Attempt

  • Check the Sender's Email Address: Look for slight misspellings or unusual domain names.
  • Look for Generic Greetings: "Dear Customer" instead of your name can be a red flag.
  • Urgent or Threatening Language: Attackers often try to create a sense of urgency or fear.
  • Suspicious Links or Attachments: Hover over links to see the actual URL. Be wary of unexpected attachments.
  • Poor Grammar and Spelling: Professional organizations usually have high standards for their communications.
  • Requests for Sensitive Information: Legitimate companies rarely ask for login credentials or financial details via email.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Use strong, unique passwords for all your accounts.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) whenever possible.
  • Keep your software and systems updated.
  • Be cautious about clicking links or downloading attachments from unknown sources.
  • Verify requests for sensitive information through a separate, trusted channel.
  • Use security software and browser extensions designed to detect phishing sites.
  • Regularly check your bank and credit card statements for suspicious activity.
